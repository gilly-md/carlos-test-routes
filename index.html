How to Use These Routes

Each route page gives you two options: a View on Google Maps link and a Download GPX file. Choose whichever works best with your setup.

Option 1: Google Maps (phone / CarPlay / Android Auto)

  • Tap View on Google Maps on your phone to open the route directly in the Google Maps app.
  • Press Start in Google Maps and it will give you turn-by-turn navigation.
  • If your car supports Apple CarPlay or Android Auto, the navigation will automatically mirror to your car's screen.

Option 2: GPX File (USB / built-in sat nav / GPS device)

  • USB import (BMW, Mercedes, VW, Audi, etc.): Copy the .gpx file to a USB stick, plug it into your car, and look for an "Import route" or "Load GPX" option in the navigation menu.
  • Garmin: Connect the device to your computer via USB. Copy the GPX file into the Garmin/GPX folder, then eject and select the route from "Where To?" > "Saved" > "Routes".
  • TomTom: Use TomTom MyDrive to import the GPX file, or copy it to the device's internal storage and import via the route planner.
  • Tesla: Tesla does not natively support GPX import. Use the Google Maps link on your phone and share the destination to the Tesla app instead.
Technical note: The GPX files use the <rte> (route) format with named <rtept> waypoints. Your car's GPS will automatically calculate the driving path between each waypoint. The waypoints mark key junctions and road changes along the test route — the detailed turn-by-turn directions are shown in the tables on each route page.
